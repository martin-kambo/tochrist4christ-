What This Is

Faith is not a destination.
It's a daily walk.

To Christ 4 Christ is a structured discipleship course built for anyone who has ever felt the pull of God's word but didn't know where to begin — or how to go deeper. Free. Interactive. At your own pace.

No prior theology required

Whether you're new in faith or returning after years away — this course meets you exactly where you are.

Built for real life

Short, focused lessons. Daily reflection prompts. No long lectures — just truth you can carry with you.

Community, not just content

You'll be connected with others on the same journey. Growth happens in relationship, not isolation.

Track your growth

See your progress, earn milestones, and revisit lessons as many times as you need. Your pace, your path.

The Course

Six modules.
One transformation.

Each module builds on the last. Complete them in order or revisit any lesson at any time.

1

The Foundation — Who Is God?

5 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Beginner

Before we can follow Jesus, we must know who He is. This module opens with the character of God as revealed in Scripture — His holiness, love, justice, and mercy — and why each of those attributes transforms the way we actually live. We'll wrestle honestly with the question "Who is God?" because it is the most important question any human being can answer. Key scriptures: Genesis 1:1; John 1:1–14; Isaiah 6:1–8; Romans 5:8.

  • In the Beginning — The God Who Was There Before Everything (Genesis 1; John 1:1)
  • The Names of God: Yahweh, Elohim, El Shaddai — and What They Reveal About His Nature
  • Holy, Holy, Holy — Understanding God's Character Through Isaiah's Vision (Isaiah 6)
  • The God Who Pursues: Grace, Redemption, and Why the Cross Changes Everything
  • The Trinity — One God, Three Persons: A Grounded, Simple Explanation

The Word — Reading Scripture With Purpose

5 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Foundational

The Bible is not just a history book — it is a living conversation between God and His people. In this module you'll learn how to read Scripture rather than simply what it says. We'll cover the basic skills of interpretation, how to study a passage in context, and how to build a reading habit that actually sticks. Key scriptures: 2 Timothy 3:16–17; Hebrews 4:12; Psalm 119:105; Joshua 1:8.

  • An Overview of the 66 Books — How the Whole Bible Tells One Story
  • Old Covenant, New Covenant: Reading Both Testaments in Their Context
  • How to Study a Passage: Observation, Interpretation, Application (the OIA Method)
  • Lectio Divina — Sacred, Slow, Contemplative Reading for a Distracted Age
  • Building a Daily Scripture Habit That Survives Busy Seasons (Practical Tools)

3

Prayer — Conversations With the Living God

4 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Intermediate

Most people know they should pray more. But something always gets in the way — distraction, doubt, the feeling that nothing is happening. In this module we diagnose why prayer feels hard and rebuild it from the ground up: not as a religious obligation, but as a genuine, life-giving conversation with a God who actually listens. Key scriptures: Matthew 6:5–13; Luke 18:1–8; Philippians 4:6–7; Psalm 62:8.

  • Why Prayer Feels Hard — And the Real Reason We Avoid It
  • The Lord's Prayer as a Daily Framework: Unpacking Matthew 6:9–13
  • ACTS: Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication — A Living Model
  • Praying Through the Psalms — Using Scripture as the Language of Prayer
  • Keeping a Prayer Journal: Structure, Prompts, and a Downloadable Template

4

Identity — Who You Are in Christ

5 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Intermediate

Every believer carries wounds from old identities — shaped by failure, comparison, family history, or the opinions of others. Scripture tells a radically different story about who you are. This module takes you through what God actually says about you in Christ, and helps you believe it — not just intellectually, but in the decisions you make and the way you carry yourself each day. Key scriptures: Ephesians 1:3–14; Romans 8:14–17; 2 Corinthians 5:17; Galatians 2:20.

  • Loved, Chosen, Adopted — Your New Identity Rooted in Ephesians 1
  • Breaking the Power of Shame: What the Cross Actually Accomplished
  • When Your Identity Clashes With Your Culture, Past, or Family Expectations
  • Calling vs Career — Discovering and Pursuing Your God-Given Purpose
  • Writing Your Identity Statement: A Personal Declaration Grounded in Scripture

5

Community — Iron Sharpens Iron

4 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Applied

The Christian life was never designed to be a solo journey. In Acts 2, the early church devoted themselves to one another — and the result was explosive growth, radical generosity, and genuine joy. This module equips you to find your place in a community of believers, and to be the kind of friend who actually makes others better. Key scriptures: Acts 2:42–47; Proverbs 27:17; Hebrews 10:24–25; Galatians 6:2.

  • The Early Church Blueprint — A Close Reading of Acts 2:42–47
  • Finding a Mentor and Being One: What Spiritual Fathering and Mothering Looks Like
  • Navigating Conflict in Christian Relationships (Matthew 18:15–17, Practically Applied)
  • Accountability Without Shame — Bearing One Another's Burdens (Galatians 6:2)
  • Building Your Personal Advisory Circle: Who Should Be Speaking Into Your Life?

6

Mission — Go and Make Disciples

5 Lessons · 2 Weeks · Advanced

The Great Commission in Matthew 28 was not addressed to pastors or career missionaries — it was given to every disciple. This final module equips you with a clear, practical framework for sharing your faith naturally, walking with others in their own journey, and multiplying what you've received. Your story becomes someone else's starting point. Key scriptures: Matthew 28:18–20; Acts 1:8; 1 Peter 3:15; 2 Timothy 2:2.

  • Understanding the Great Commission for Today — What "Go" Actually Means
  • Your Sphere of Influence: Mission Starts at Home, Work, and Your Neighbourhood
  • Sharing Your Faith Without Scripts, Pressure, or Awkwardness — Real Conversations
  • How to Lead a Small Discipleship Group: Structure, Questions, and Free Resources
  • Graduation: Writing Your Personal Mission Statement and Committing It to Prayer
